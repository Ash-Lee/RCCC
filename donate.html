Friends of Rotherham Cancer Care Centre

The generosity of service users and the local community has in no doubt contributed to the success of the Centre and although many people leave a donation or raise money with their friends for the Centre, many people wish to stay in touch and continue to support RCCC.

Who are our friends?

Our friends are mostly people who have a connection to RCCC either by accessing the service, via a diagnosis of cancer, or as a carer for someone living with cancer. Our friends are people who care enough to want to help us to ensure the future of RCCC for the people of Rotherham.

What do friends do?

Our friends receive a welcome pack and regular updates on RCCC with details of upcoming events, and some friends volunteer their time to help at certain events. Our friends include people who simply wish to stay in contact with the Centre and receive information about how the Centre is developing, fundraise in the community and/or fundraise at work or amongst their families.

Friends who are unable to fundraise themselves, help us by being aware of local groups, churches and organisations who may help local charities and either recommend RCCC to the groups or contact RCCC to make us aware. Some of our friends also wish to become members of RCCC, which entitles them to an invite to our annual AGM and to have the right to vote for or against for members of our Board.

How much does it cost to become a friend of RCCC?

There is no financial commitment to becoming a friend of RCCC.

How much does it cost to become a member of RCCC?

Members pay a monthly donation of £2.00 paid by standing order or an annual payment of £24. Most important of all, our friends remain supportive and interested in the future of RCCC. Everyone who gets involved makes a real difference to RCCC and to residents in Rotherham who are living with cancer.

How can I become a friend of RCCC?

It’s easy to join Friends – just contact the Centre by email to receive further information. If you are visiting the Centre, you can pick up a Friends membership sign-up form from a member of staff.
